My Past

Where I am from

Chin State of Myanmar

I am from Bungtlang, Chin state of Myanmar. A every ethnically and linguistically diverse part of the country. Whilst being part of Myanmar, the people of Chin state are not the same ethnically to the Bamar which make up ~70% of the country.

The Chin People

The Chin people are a diverse ethnic group of approximately 1.5 million people primarily residing in the mountainous Chin State of western Myanmar. Unlike the Buddhist Bamar majority, the Chin are predominantly Christian—a legacy of 19th-century American missionaries—and they view this faith as a core pillar of their identity.

Accross the globe

Socially, the Chin identity is an umbrella for over 50 distinct tribes, each with its own language and unique customs. Today, due to long-standing political conflict and religious persecution, a significant Chin diaspora exists in India, Malaysia, and the United States. I am among the few that had the privilege to enter the US.

Cybersecurity

I am currently majoring in Cybersecurity; it is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. These attacks usually aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information; extort money (ransomware); or interrupt normal business operations. I do enjoy learning and applying this into my own life. I believe it is a field that has a lot of opportunities, and I am doing something that positively impacts my world.
